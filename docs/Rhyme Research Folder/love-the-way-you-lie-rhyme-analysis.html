üéØ Key Techniques for Your Suno Templates:

1. Compound Rhyme Chains: Plan entire sections where "-it 'em" or "-ound" endings repeat across 4-6 lines. Creates hypnotic flow.

2. Dual Assonance: Run two vowel sounds simultaneously (long A + long O). One catches attention, the other creates subliminal cohesion.

3. Build to Wordplay: Use 3-4 lines of setup rhymes, then land a pun or double-meaning as the payoff (like "windowpane").

4. Monorhyme for Intensity: 4+ lines ending in the same sound creates obsessive, urgent energy.

5. Embedded Rhymes: Hide rhyme words inside longer words ("apologize" contains "lies"). Rewards close listening.

6. Imperfect Endings: End a verse on a slant rhyme to create unresolved tension that carries into the next section.
